1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
Build a complete, production-ready mobile application called HomeChef — a homemade food marketplace that connects home-based cooks, bakers, and dessert makers with local customers looking for authentic, handcrafted meals.
This is NOT a restaurant or grocery delivery app. It is a personal, community-driven platform where home cooks share what they make, and customers discover real food from real people nearby.
The UI design is fully provided via the attached Stitch design file. Your job is to implement it completely — pixel-perfect — with all business logic, navigation flows, state management, real-time updates, and API integrations.
Core Concept
A home baker who makes donuts or tiramisu has no storefront — they currently rely on social media and word of mouth. HomeChef gives them a professional platform to:
Post what they are making today with photos, price, and available quantity
Receive preparation requests for dishes they can cook on demand
Showcase their full specialty menu for advance pre-orders
Manage orders, earnings, and customer relationships in one place
Customers get to:
Discover homemade food nearby updated daily
Order before it sells out with real-time availability
Request custom preparation for a future date
Pre-order from a chef's specialty menu for any occasion

4. THE THREE ORDER MODES (CORE BUSINESS LOGIC)
This is the heart of the application. Three distinct ordering modes must coexist seamlessly within the same platform.
Mode 1 — Instant Order (Daily Post)
The chef posts what they have prepared today. Customers order from what is currently available.
Chef publishes a daily post with photos, price, available quantity, and order deadline
Post appears live in all followers' feeds and nearby discovery feed
Real-time remaining quantity counter counts down as orders come in
Countdown timer shows time remaining until order deadline
When quantity reaches zero or deadline passes, post automatically shows Sold Out or Closed
Payment: Pay Now via card or Pay on Delivery
Mode 2 — Preparation Request
The chef lists what they are capable of preparing. The customer requests a specific dish for a future date and the chef approves or rejects.
Chef maintains a Preparation Menu — a list of dishes they can cook on request
Each item in the prep menu has: name, description, photos, base price, minimum order quantity, minimum notice time in hours
Customer browses the prep menu on the chef's profile and selects a dish
Customer fills a request form: desired date and time, quantity, special instructions, and budget if negotiable
Chef receives push notification with full request details
Chef can: Accept with confirmed price and time, Reject with an optional reason, or Counter-offer with a different price or date
Customer receives push notification with the chef's response
If accepted: order is created and payment is processed or scheduled
Full conversation thread between customer and chef on the request
Mode 3 — Specialty Pre-Order
The chef's profile displays everything they are known for. Customers browse this catalog and place advance orders for any item regardless of whether it was posted today.
Chef maintains a Specialties Catalog — their complete portfolio of offerings
Each specialty has: photos, description, price range, typical preparation time, availability (always available / seasonal / on request)
Customer taps any specialty item and selects a future date from a calendar picker
System checks chef's availability calendar and blocks unavailable dates
Order is submitted as a pre-order and chef confirms or suggests an alternative date
Ideal for occasions: birthdays, gatherings, events — where planning ahead is necessary

8. ADVANCED FEATURES
Flash Sales
Chef can create a time-limited discount on any daily post
Discount percentage and start and end time
Post shows Flash Sale badge with countdown timer in feed
Original price shown crossed out with discounted price
All nearby users who follow that chef receive push notification
Group Orders
Customer creates a group order for a prep menu item with a target quantity
Shares a link or invite code with friends
Participants join and add their individual quantities
When target quantity is reached, group order is submitted to chef
All participants receive updates on group progress and order status
Chef sees group order as a single order with total quantity
Waitlist
When a daily post sells out, customer can join the waitlist
If another customer cancels, first person on waitlist receives immediate push notification
Notification includes a deep link to the post with a 15-minute window to order
If they do not order within 15 minutes, next person on waitlist is notified
Weekly Subscriptions
Customer subscribes to a specific chef for a recurring weekly or biweekly order
Customer selects preferred day, quantity, and item from prep menu or specialties
Chef receives automatic prep request each cycle
Subscriber gets a loyalty discount negotiated with chef
Customer can pause or cancel subscription at any time
Coming Soon — Teaser Posts
Chef posts a photo or description of what they will make tomorrow
Customers tap Interested button
When chef publishes the actual post, all interested customers receive push notification
Teasers appear on chef profile with Interested count
Smart Price Suggestions for Chefs
When creating a post or prep menu item, app suggests a price range
Based on similar dishes in the same area and category
Shown as a gentle tip — chef can accept or ignore
Dispute Resolution
Customer opens a dispute if order did not arrive, was wrong, or had quality issues
Customer selects reason from list and adds photos as evidence
Platform holds payment until dispute is resolved
Chef is notified and can respond within 24 hours
If no response within 24 hours, refund is issued automatically
Platform admin can intervene on escalated disputes
